GLOBEX CORPORATION — DATA PRIVACY POLICY v2.4
Document ID: DP-POL-001 | Effective: January 15, 2026
Classification: Confidential | Owner: Data Protection Officer
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. PURPOSE & SCOPE
───────────────────
1.1 This policy governs the collection, processing, storage, and disposal of personal data by Globex Corporation and its subsidiaries.
1.2 Applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who handle personal data on behalf of Globex.
1.3 Compliance with GDPR (EU), DPDPA 2023 (India), CCPA (California), and PDPA (Singapore) is mandatory.

2. DEFINITIONS
───────────────
2.1 Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person — name, email, phone number, employee ID, IP address, location data, biometric data.
2.2 Sensitive Personal Data: Financial information, health records, sexual orientation, political opinions, religious beliefs, biometric data, genetic data.
2.3 Data Subject: The individual whose personal data is being processed.
2.4 Data Controller: Globex Corporation (determines purpose and means of processing).
2.5 Data Processor: Any third party processing data on behalf of Globex.

3. DATA COLLECTION PRINCIPLES
──────────────────────────────
3.1 LAWFUL BASIS: Personal data must only be collected with a valid legal basis — consent, contractual necessity, legal obligation, or legitimate interest.
3.2 PURPOSE LIMITATION: Data collected for one purpose must not be repurposed without additional consent.
3.3 DATA MINIMIZATION: Only the minimum necessary data should be collected. Over-collection is prohibited.
3.4 ACCURACY: Data must be kept accurate and up-to-date. Mechanisms for data subjects to request corrections must be provided.

4. CONSENT MANAGEMENT
──────────────────────
4.1 Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous.
4.2 Pre-ticked consent boxes are prohibited. Consent must be an affirmative action.
4.3 Consent withdrawal must be as easy as providing consent. Processing must cease within 72 hours of withdrawal.
4.4 Records of consent (who, when, what, how) must be maintained for the full retention period plus 2 years.

5. DATA RETENTION & DISPOSAL
─────────────────────────────
5.1 Retention Periods:
    - Employee records: 7 years after separation
    - Client project data: 5 years after project closure
    - Marketing consent data: Duration of consent + 2 years
    - Financial transactions: 10 years (regulatory requirement)
    - Website analytics: 26 months (GDPR standard)
5.2 Data past its retention period must be securely deleted within 30 days.
5.3 Deletion Methods:
    - Digital: Cryptographic erasure or NIST 800-88 compliant wiping
    - Physical: Cross-cut shredding (DIN 66399 Level P-4 or higher)

6. CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFER
──────────────────────────────
6.1 Personal data of EU residents must not be transferred outside the EEA without Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or an adequacy decision.
6.2 Personal data of Indian citizens must be stored within India (mirror copy), per DPDPA 2023 requirements.
6.3 Transfer Impact Assessments (TIA) must be completed before any new cross-border data flow.
6.4 Approved transfer mechanisms: SCCs, Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs), or adequacy decisions.

7. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
───────────────────────
7.1 Right to Access: Data subjects can request a copy of their personal data. Response within 30 days.
7.2 Right to Rectification: Corrections must be made within 15 business days.
7.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Erasure requests must be fulfilled within 30 days, unless legal retention obligations override.
7.4 Right to Portability: Data must be provided in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).
7.5 Right to Object: Data subjects may object to processing based on legitimate interest. Processing must cease pending review.

8. BREACH NOTIFICATION
───────────────────────
8.1 Data breaches must be reported to the Data Protection Officer within 24 hours of discovery.
8.2 DPO Contact: dpo@globex-corp.com | Direct: +91-80-4455-7800
8.3 Regulatory notification (to relevant Data Protection Authority) must occur within 72 hours per GDPR Article 33.
8.4 Affected data subjects must be notified "without undue delay" if the breach is likely to result in high risk to their rights.

9. AI & AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING
───────────────────────────────────
9.1 Automated decisions that significantly affect individuals (credit scoring, hiring recommendations, performance ratings) must include human oversight.
9.2 Data subjects have the right to request human review of automated decisions per GDPR Article 22.
9.3 AI models trained on personal data must document: data sources, training methodology, bias testing results, and accuracy metrics.
9.4 Profiling for marketing purposes requires explicit opt-in consent.

DOCUMENT TYPE: Internal Enterprise Policy
POLICY: AI/ML Model Governance Policy
VERSION: 1.0
EFFECTIVE DATE: 2025-03-01
LAST REVIEWED: 2025-09-15
OWNER: Chief Data Officer (CDO) / AI Ethics Committee
APPLICABILITY: All AI and Machine Learning models developed, deployed, or procured by the organization

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — MODEL INVENTORY AND CLASSIFICATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Model Registry: All AI/ML models in development or production must be registered in the central Model Registry with: model name, purpose, owner, training data sources, deployment date, and current status.

1.2 Risk Classification: Models are classified based on their impact:
    (a) HIGH IMPACT: Models making or influencing decisions about individuals (credit scoring, insurance pricing, hiring, medical diagnosis)
    (b) MEDIUM IMPACT: Models used for operational optimization (demand forecasting, inventory management, fraud detection)
    (c) LOW IMPACT: Models used for internal productivity (document classification, search ranking, chatbots for internal use)

1.3 Current Model Inventory:
    — Credit Risk Scoring Model (HIGH IMPACT) — deployed in Payment Gateway
    — Customer Churn Prediction Model (MEDIUM IMPACT) — deployed in CRM System
    — Product Recommendation Engine (MEDIUM IMPACT) — deployed in Customer Portal
    — Document Classification Model (LOW IMPACT) — deployed internally
    — Fraud Detection Model (HIGH IMPACT) — deployed in Payment Gateway

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — DEVELOPMENT STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Training Data: 
    (a) Training data must be documented with data provenance and lineage
    (b) Training data must be reviewed for bias before model training begins
    (c) Personal data used for training SHOULD be anonymized, but pseudonymized data is currently accepted for HIGH IMPACT models where anonymization degrades model accuracy
    — NOTE: Full anonymization for training data is a goal but not yet a requirement.

2.2 Bias Testing: All HIGH IMPACT models must undergo bias testing across protected attributes (gender, age, ethnicity, religion) before deployment. Bias test results must be documented and approved by the AI Ethics Committee.

2.3 Model Documentation: Each model must have a Model Card containing: intended use, training methodology, performance metrics, known limitations, and failure modes.
    — NOTE: Model Cards are currently required for HIGH IMPACT models only. MEDIUM and LOW IMPACT models have optional documentation.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — DEPLOYMENT AND MONITORING
═══════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Approval Gate: HIGH IMPACT models require sign-off from the AI Ethics Committee and the relevant business unit head before production deployment.

3.2 Human Oversight: HIGH IMPACT models operate in a "human-in-the-loop" mode where outputs are reviewed by a qualified human before final decisions are communicated to customers.
    — NOTE: The Fraud Detection Model operates in "human-on-the-loop" mode (automated decisions with human review of flagged edge cases) due to latency requirements. This exception was approved by the AI Ethics Committee on 2025-05-20.

3.3 Model Monitoring: Production models are monitored for performance drift quarterly. Retraining is triggered when accuracy drops below the documented threshold.
    — NOTE: Real-time drift monitoring is not yet implemented. Drift detection relies on quarterly manual evaluation.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 4 — TRANSPARENCY AND EXPLAINABILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 Customer Notification: Customers are currently NOT explicitly notified when an AI system is involved in processing their request or making a decision affecting them.
    — NOTE: A customer notification mechanism is planned for Q4 2026.

4.2 Explainability: HIGH IMPACT models must provide feature-level explanations (e.g., SHAP values) for individual decisions when requested by a customer or regulator.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 5 — THIRD-PARTY AI MODELS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 Procured AI models (e.g., vendor APIs, SaaS AI features) must undergo the same risk classification and governance as internally developed models.
5.2 Vendor AI model documentation must be obtained and stored in the Model Registry.
5.3 The organization reserves the right to audit vendor AI model performance and bias metrics.

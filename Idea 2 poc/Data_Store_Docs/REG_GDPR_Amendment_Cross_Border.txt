DOCUMENT TYPE: Regulatory Amendment
REGULATION: General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — Supplementary Rules on Cross-Border Data Transfers
ISSUING AUTHORITY: European Data Protection Board (EDPB)
REFERENCE: Guidelines 05/2025 on Cross-Border Data Transfers under Chapter V GDPR
DATE OF ADOPTION: October 15, 2025
EFFECTIVE DATE: January 15, 2026
JURISDICTION: European Union / European Economic Area (EEA)
APPLICABILITY: All controllers and processors transferring personal data of EEA residents to third countries

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — ENHANCED TRANSFER IMPACT ASSESSMENTS (TIA)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Mandatory TIA: Before any transfer of EEA personal data to a third country, the data exporter MUST conduct a documented Transfer Impact Assessment evaluating:
    (a) The legal framework of the recipient country regarding government access to data
    (b) The specific categories of personal data being transferred
    (c) The technical and organizational measures in place to protect the data
    (d) Whether the recipient country provides "essentially equivalent" protection to GDPR

1.2 TIA Review Cycle: Transfer Impact Assessments must be reviewed and updated:
    (a) At least annually
    (b) Whenever there is a material change in the recipient country's legal framework
    (c) Whenever the categories of data being transferred change

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — STANDARD CONTRACTUAL CLAUSES (SCCs) — UPDATED REQUIREMENTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 New Modular SCCs: All existing data transfer agreements using the 2021 SCCs must be updated to incorporate the new Supplementary Module (Module 5) addressing:
    (a) Government access notification obligations
    (b) Enhanced encryption-in-transit requirements (minimum TLS 1.3)
    (c) Data breach notification to the exporter within 24 hours (reduced from 72 hours)

2.2 Sub-Processor Chain: Data importers must maintain a complete, auditable chain of all sub-processors involved in processing the transferred data. Any sub-processor in a country without an adequacy decision must independently meet TIA requirements.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — SUPPLEMENTARY MEASURES
═══════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Technical Measures (mandatory for all transfers):
    (a) End-to-end encryption with keys controlled exclusively by the data exporter
    (b) Pseudonymization at the point of export where feasible
    (c) Access controls ensuring the data importer cannot decrypt data without exporter authorization (where technically possible)

3.2 Organizational Measures (mandatory):
    (a) Data importer must notify the exporter of any legally binding government access request before complying (unless prohibited by law)
    (b) Data importer must challenge government access requests that are disproportionate
    (c) Annual transparency report on government access requests received

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 4 — SPECIFIC COUNTRY GUIDANCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 Countries with Adequacy Decision: Transfers proceed under normal GDPR rules. Current list includes: Japan, South Korea, United Kingdom, Canada (commercial organizations), Argentina, Israel, New Zealand, Switzerland, Uruguay.

4.2 Countries WITHOUT Adequacy Decision (requiring SCC + TIA + Supplementary Measures): United States, India, Brazil, China, UAE, South Africa, and all others not listed above.

4.3 Countries where transfer is HIGH RISK (requiring enhanced due diligence): Countries with broad government surveillance powers or without independent data protection authorities.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 5 — ENFORCEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 Supervisory authorities will prioritize enforcement actions against transfers lacking proper TIAs.
5.2 Organizations found transferring data without adequate safeguards face fines up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover under GDPR Article 83.
5.3 Supervisory authorities may issue emergency suspension orders for ongoing transfers deemed to pose immediate risk to data subjects.

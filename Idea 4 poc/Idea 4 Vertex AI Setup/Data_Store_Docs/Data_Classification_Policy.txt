SECTION 1 — DATA CLASSIFICATION TIERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

All data processed by the organization must be classified into one of four tiers:

TIER 1 — PUBLIC
Definition: Information intended for public disclosure.
Examples: Marketing materials, public API documentation, press releases.
Handling: No restrictions on storage or transmission.

TIER 2 — INTERNAL
Definition: Information intended for internal use only, not harmful if disclosed but not intended for external audiences.
Examples: Internal wikis, engineering documentation, architecture diagrams, non-sensitive meeting notes.
Handling: Must be stored on approved corporate systems. Must not be shared on personal devices or public cloud storage.

TIER 3 — CONFIDENTIAL
Definition: Sensitive business information that would cause competitive or financial harm if disclosed.
Examples: Business strategies, financial projections, vendor contract terms, pre-release product plans, employee compensation data.
Handling: Must be encrypted at rest (AES-256 with CMEK). Access restricted to named individuals with business need. Must not be sent via unencrypted email. Third-party sharing requires Legal approval and NDA.

TIER 4 — RESTRICTED
Definition: Highly sensitive data subject to regulatory requirements. Unauthorized disclosure would result in regulatory penalties, legal liability, or significant harm to individuals.
Examples: Customer PII (names, emails, phone numbers, national IDs), financial transaction data, payment card data, health records, authentication credentials, encryption keys.
Handling: Must be encrypted at rest AND in transit. Access requires MFA and JIT provisioning. Must NOT appear in logs (see Post-Mortem PII Leak 2025-01). Must be pseudonymized for analytics/ML (see GDPR Addendum, Article 4). Processing location restricted per data residency policy. Third-party access requires DPO approval, DPA, and SCCs.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — VENDOR ACCESS BY DATA TIER
═══════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Tier 1 (Public): Any vendor may process.
2.2 Tier 2 (Internal): Vendors with signed NDA may process.
2.3 Tier 3 (Confidential): Vendors with NDA + SOC2 certification may process. Requires VP approval.
2.4 Tier 4 (Restricted): Vendors with NDA + SOC2 + ISO 27001 + executed DPA with SCCs. Requires DPO and CISO approval. Vendor must appear on the approved Sub-Processor Register (see GDPR Addendum, Article 8).

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — CLASSIFICATION RESPONSIBILITIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Data owners (product/service teams) are responsible for classifying data at the point of creation or collection.

3.2 When data classification is unclear, default to the HIGHER tier until clarified by the CISO or DPO.

3.3 Data classification must be reviewed annually or when data handling processes change.

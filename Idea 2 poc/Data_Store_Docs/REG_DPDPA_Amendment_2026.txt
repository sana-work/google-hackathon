DOCUMENT TYPE: Regulatory Circular
REGULATION: Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) — Amendment Notification 2026
ISSUING AUTHORITY: Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India
NOTIFICATION NUMBER: S.O. 1247(E)
DATE OF NOTIFICATION: January 15, 2026
EFFECTIVE DATE: April 15, 2026 (90-day compliance window)
JURISDICTION: Republic of India
APPLICABILITY: All Data Fiduciaries and Data Processors handling personal data of Indian citizens

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — AMENDMENTS TO DATA RETENTION (Chapter IV, Section 8)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Maximum Retention Period: Personal data collected for service delivery purposes SHALL NOT be retained beyond 3 years from the date of last interaction with the Data Principal, unless a longer retention is mandated by a sector-specific regulator (e.g., RBI, SEBI, IRDAI).
    — PREVIOUS RULE: 5-year maximum retention was permitted.
    — CHANGE: Reduced to 3 years. All existing data exceeding the new limit must be purged within the 90-day compliance window.

1.2 Retention Justification: Data Fiduciaries MUST maintain a documented justification for retaining personal data beyond 1 year. This justification must be reviewed annually by the Data Protection Officer (DPO) and made available to the Data Protection Board upon request.

1.3 Automated Deletion: All systems storing personal data MUST implement automated deletion mechanisms that trigger purging once the retention period expires. Manual-only deletion processes are no longer acceptable.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — CONSENT MANAGEMENT (Chapter II, Section 6)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Explicit Opt-In: Data Fiduciaries MUST obtain explicit, affirmative opt-in consent from Data Principals before processing personal data for marketing, analytics, or profiling purposes. Pre-checked consent boxes, implied consent, or bundled consent are PROHIBITED.
    — PREVIOUS RULE: Opt-out model was acceptable for non-sensitive data.
    — CHANGE: All processing now requires opt-in consent. Existing opt-out consents are invalid as of the effective date.

2.2 Consent Granularity: Consent MUST be collected separately for each processing purpose (e.g., service delivery, marketing, analytics, third-party sharing). A single blanket consent covering multiple purposes is NOT valid.

2.3 Consent Withdrawal: Data Principals must be able to withdraw consent through the same channel used to provide it, within a maximum of 48 hours of request submission.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFER (Chapter IV, Section 16)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Approved Jurisdictions: Personal data of Indian citizens may only be transferred to jurisdictions explicitly approved by the Central Government via notification. As of this amendment, the approved list includes: European Union member states, United Kingdom, Japan, and Singapore.
    — IMPORTANT: United States is NOT on the approved list unless a bilateral data protection agreement is executed.

3.2 Data Localization for Sensitive Data: Sensitive personal data (financial records, health records, biometric data) MUST be stored and processed exclusively within the territory of India. No exceptions for cloud provider mirroring or disaster recovery.

3.3 Third-Party Transfer Notice: Any transfer of personal data to a third-party processor (domestic or international) MUST be disclosed to the Data Principal at the time of consent collection.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 4 — PENALTIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 Non-compliance with data retention limits: Up to ₹50 Crore per instance.
4.2 Failure to implement proper consent mechanisms: Up to ₹100 Crore per instance.
4.3 Unauthorized cross-border transfer: Up to ₹250 Crore per instance.
4.4 Failure to implement automated deletion: Up to ₹10 Crore per instance.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 5 — COMPLIANCE TIMELINE
═══════════════════════════════════════════

All Data Fiduciaries must achieve full compliance by April 15, 2026 (90 days from notification date).
Data Fiduciaries with more than 10 million Data Principal records may apply for a 30-day extension (maximum deadline: May 15, 2026) by submitting a compliance roadmap to the Data Protection Board.

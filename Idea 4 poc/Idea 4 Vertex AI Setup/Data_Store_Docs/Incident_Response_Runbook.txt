SECTION 1 – SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION
- SEV-1 (Critical): Complete service outage or data breach affecting >10,000 users. Response time: 15 minutes. War room activation: Mandatory.
- SEV-2 (Major): Partial degradation affecting core functionality (e.g., payments, authentication). Response time: 30 minutes.
- SEV-3 (Minor): Non-critical feature degradation. Response time: 4 hours.
- SEV-4 (Low): Cosmetic issues or non-user-facing bugs. Response time: Next business day.

SECTION 2 – MANDATORY ESCALATION RULES
1. Any incident involving the Payment Processing API or Authentication Service is automatically classified as SEV-2 or higher.
2. If a SEV-1 or SEV-2 incident is not resolved within 60 minutes, it MUST be escalated to the VP of Engineering.
3. Any incident suspected of involving a data breach MUST immediately trigger the GDPR breach notification process (see GDPR Data Processing Addendum, Article 5).

SECTION 3 – POST-INCIDENT REVIEW
1. All SEV-1 and SEV-2 incidents require a blameless post-mortem document within 5 business days.
2. All post-mortem action items must be tracked in Jira with a target resolution of 14 days.
3. Root causes must be classified using the "5 Whys" methodology.

DOCUMENT: GDPR & DPDPA CUSTOMER DATA COMPLIANCE POLICY
CLASSIFICATION: INTERNAL — LEGAL/COMPLIANCE
EFFECTIVE DATE: January 1, 2025
OWNER: Chief Privacy Officer & Data Protection Officer
================================================================================

1. PURPOSE
   Ensures all customer behavioral data collection, processing, and
   retention for the Silent Escalation Predictor complies with GDPR
   (EU), DPDPA (India), and other applicable data protection regulations.

2. LAWFUL BASIS FOR PROCESSING
   2.1 Behavioral data processing for churn prediction is conducted under
       LEGITIMATE INTEREST basis (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)) with documented
       Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) on file.
   2.2 Customers are informed about behavioral analytics in the Privacy
       Policy and Terms of Service (Section 4.3: "Service Improvement
       and Customer Experience Analytics").

3. DATA MINIMIZATION PRINCIPLES
   3.1 Only AGGREGATED behavioral metrics are used — never raw activity logs
   3.2 No personally identifiable information (PII) enters the prediction model
   3.3 Customer data is pseudonymized using internal customer IDs
   3.4 Sensitive categories (religion, health, political) are NEVER collected

4. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS (GDPR/DPDPA)
   4.1 RIGHT TO ACCESS: Customers can request their behavioral profile
       and risk score through the Customer Portal within 30 days
   4.2 RIGHT TO RECTIFICATION: Incorrect data corrected within 72 hours
   4.3 RIGHT TO ERASURE: Customer data deleted from prediction system
       within 30 days of valid request (except legal retention requirements)
   4.4 RIGHT TO OBJECT: Customers may opt out of predictive analytics;
       their data will be excluded from future scoring cycles
   4.5 RIGHT TO PORTABILITY: Behavioral data exportable in CSV format

5. DATA RETENTION SCHEDULE
   - Active customer behavioral signals: Retained for duration of contract
   - Post-churn behavioral data: Anonymized and retained for 24 months
     (for model training purposes only)
   - Intervention logs: Retained for 36 months (audit requirements)
   - After retention period: Data permanently deleted or fully anonymized

6. CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFERS
   6.1 Customer behavioral data stored in regional data centers:
       - EU customers: europe-west1 (Belgium)
       - India customers: asia-south1 (Mumbai)
       - US customers: us-central1 (Iowa)
   6.2 No cross-border transfer without Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)

7. AI-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE
   7.1 The Silent Escalation Predictor does NOT make automated decisions
       that produce legal effects. It provides recommendations only.
   7.2 HIGH RISK interventions ALWAYS require human-in-the-loop approval
   7.3 The system provides explainable predictions with evidence — never
       opaque scoring
   7.4 Regular bias audits conducted quarterly across customer demographics
   7.5 Model cards and fairness reports maintained and available for audit

8. BREACH RESPONSE
   - Any data breach affecting behavioral data: Notify DPO within 4 hours
   - Regulatory notification (GDPR): Within 72 hours if customer data affected
   - Customer notification: Within 7 days if high risk to rights and freedoms

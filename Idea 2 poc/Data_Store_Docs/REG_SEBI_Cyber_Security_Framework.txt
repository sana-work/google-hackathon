DOCUMENT TYPE: Regulatory Framework
REGULATION: SEBI Cyber Security and Cyber Resilience Framework for Market Infrastructure Institutions and Regulated Entities
CIRCULAR NUMBER: SEBI/HO/ITD/ITD-SEC-1/P/CIR/2025/198
DATE OF ISSUANCE: December 10, 2025
EFFECTIVE DATE: March 10, 2026 (90-day compliance window)
JURISDICTION: Republic of India
APPLICABILITY: Stock Exchanges, Depositories, Clearing Corporations, Mutual Fund AMCs, Brokerage Firms, Portfolio Managers, and their technology service providers

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — INCIDENT REPORTING
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1.1 Mandatory Reporting: All cyber security incidents — including attempted breaches, successful breaches, data leaks, ransomware attacks, DDoS attacks, and insider threat events — MUST be reported to SEBI within 6 hours of detection.
    — PREVIOUS RULE: 48-hour reporting window.
    — CHANGE: Reduced to 6 hours. This applies to ALL incidents regardless of severity.

1.2 Incident Classification: Entities must classify incidents as:
    (a) CRITICAL: Data breach involving investor PII, trade data, or financial records
    (b) HIGH: System unavailability exceeding 30 minutes during market hours
    (c) MEDIUM: Attempted breach blocked by security controls
    (d) LOW: Policy violation without external impact

1.3 Root Cause Analysis: A detailed Root Cause Analysis (RCA) must be submitted to SEBI within 14 days of any CRITICAL or HIGH incident.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 2 — VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Patching Timelines: 
    (a) CRITICAL vulnerabilities (CVSS ≥ 9.0): Patch within 72 hours
    (b) HIGH vulnerabilities (CVSS 7.0-8.9): Patch within 7 days
    (c) MEDIUM vulnerabilities (CVSS 4.0-6.9): Patch within 30 days
    (d) LOW vulnerabilities (CVSS < 4.0): Patch within 90 days

2.2 Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT): 
    (a) Internal VAPT: Quarterly
    (b) External VAPT by CERT-IN empanelled auditor: Semi-annually
    (c) Red team exercises: Annually

2.3 Zero-Day Response: For zero-day vulnerabilities affecting market-critical systems, entities must implement compensating controls within 24 hours even if a patch is not yet available.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 3 — ACCESS CONTROL AND IDENTITY MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is MANDATORY for:
    (a) All administrative access to production systems
    (b) All remote access (VPN, remote desktop)
    (c) All access to systems handling investor data or trade data
    (d) All privileged user accounts

3.2 Privileged Access Management (PAM): Entities must implement a PAM solution for all privileged accounts with:
    (a) Just-in-time access provisioning (no standing privileges)
    (b) Session recording for all privileged sessions
    (c) Quarterly access reviews with documented evidence

3.3 Service Account Controls: All service accounts must be inventoried, have defined owners, and undergo annual credential rotation.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 4 — SOC (SECURITY OPERATIONS CENTER)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 24x7 SOC: All Market Infrastructure Institutions (MIIs) and Category I entities MUST operate a 24x7 Security Operations Center.

4.2 SOC Capabilities: The SOC must include real-time monitoring, SIEM correlation, threat intelligence feeds, automated alerting, and documented escalation procedures.

4.3 SOC Staffing: SOC must be staffed by certified professionals (CISSP, CISM, or equivalent) at all times.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 5 — PENALTIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 Failure to report incidents within 6 hours: ₹1 Crore per incident + potential trading suspension.
5.2 Failure to meet patching timelines: ₹25 Lakh per unpatched critical vulnerability per day.
5.3 Non-implementation of MFA: ₹5 Crore + mandatory third-party audit at entity's expense.

DOCUMENT: COMMUNICATION PREFERENCES & OPT-OUT GUIDELINES
CLASSIFICATION: INTERNAL — COMPLIANCE
EFFECTIVE DATE: March 1, 2025
OWNER: Chief Privacy Officer
================================================================================

1. PURPOSE
   Defines the rules governing all outbound customer communications,
   including retention interventions, ensuring compliance with GDPR,
   DPDPA, CAN-SPAM, and internal privacy standards.

2. COMMUNICATION CHANNEL HIERARCHY
   Priority order for retention outreach (use highest available):
   1. In-app notification (least intrusive, highest engagement)
   2. Email (standard channel, requires opt-in)
   3. SMS (requires explicit consent, use for HIGH RISK only)
   4. Phone call (requires prior relationship, manager-level only)

3. OPT-OUT RULES
   3.1 Customers who have opted out of marketing emails MAY still receive
       transactional/service communications, but NOT retention campaigns.
   3.2 Customers who have opted out of ALL communications must be flagged
       as "Contact Restricted". Only billing/legal notices permitted.
   3.3 Opt-out requests must be processed within 24 hours.
   3.4 Retention interventions MUST check opt-out status before triggering.

4. FREQUENCY CAPS
   - LOW RISK: Maximum 2 retention emails per 30 days
   - MEDIUM RISK: Maximum 1 email + 1 in-app per 14 days
   - HIGH RISK: Maximum 1 phone + 1 email per 7 days
   - POST-INTERVENTION: 14-day cooling period before next outreach

5. PERSONALIZATION REQUIREMENTS
   5.1 All retention messages MUST reference specific behavioral context
   5.2 Generic bulk templates are PROHIBITED for retention interventions
   5.3 Messages must include easy opt-out link
   5.4 Tone must be empathetic, not aggressive or guilt-inducing

6. DOCUMENTATION
   Every outbound retention communication must be logged in CRM with:
   - Customer ID, channel, timestamp, message content, trigger reason
   - Customer response (if any) within 7 days
   - Intervention outcome classification at 30-day mark
